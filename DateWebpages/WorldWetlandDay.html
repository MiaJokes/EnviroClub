Welcome To World Wetland Day!

Celebrate the importance of wetlands for both the environment and human wellbeing on February 2nd!

What Are Wetlands?

Wetlands are unique semi-aquatic ecosystems, meaning they are areas where water covers the soil for most of the year. These incredible landscapes act as "Nature's Kidneys" , filtering and purifying water. They also provide a safe habitat for wildlife - offering food, shelter, and breeding grounds for countless species.

Wetlands are also essential for humans too! They help prevent floods by absorbing excess water, store carbon to combat Climate Change, and support livelihoods around the world. From the vast mangroves lining tropical coasts to tranquil inland marshes, wetlands are vital for the health of our planet.

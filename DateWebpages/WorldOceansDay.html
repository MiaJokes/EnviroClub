What Makes Our Oceans Breath?

The ocean, often dubbed the "lungs of the planet", is a vital oxygen producer. Tiny marine plants, primarily phytoplankton, harness sunlight through photosynthesis, converting carbon dioxide into oxygen. These microscopic organisms generate over half of the Earth's oxygen, making them indispensable to life as we know it. Their role in regulating our climate, absorbing carbon dioxide, and supporting marine biodiversity is irreplaceable.

However, human activities such as pollution, overfishing, and climate change pose significant threats to the health of our oceans. To safeguard this precious resource, we must adopt sustainable practices, reduce our carbon footprint, and minimize plastic pollution. By protecting our oceans, we ensure a healthy planet for future generations.

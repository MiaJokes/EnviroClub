Why Is Wildlife In Danger?

Wildlife is in danger because of several serious threats, many of which are caused by human activities. Habitat loss is a primary issue, as deforestation, urbanization, and agriculture destroy the natural spaces animals depend on for survival. Climate change is rapidly worsening these threats by shifting weather patterns and disrupting ecosystems, forcing animals to adapt quickly — or risk extinction.

Pollution also plays a huge role, with plastics, chemicals, and other waste harming wildlife both on land and in the oceans. Overfishing, poaching, and the illegal wildlife trade are pushing many species to the brink. On top of that, invasive species introduced by humans are upsetting the balance of ecosystems and threatening native wildlife. These problems are all connected, and if we don't take action soon, we could lose many of the species that are essential to the health of our planet.
